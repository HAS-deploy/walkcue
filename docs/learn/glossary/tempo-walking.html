tempo walking — walking workout glossary

What it means

Tempo walking is a sustained, challenging pace held for a set period — typically 20–40 minutes — at an intensity that feels "comfortably hard." It sits between a brisk everyday walk and a full-effort race walk. The goal is to stay at roughly 70–80% of your maximum heart rate, the point where you can still speak in short phrases but wouldn't want to hold a full conversation. Think of it as the walking equivalent of a tempo run in running training.

The pace varies by fitness level, but most people find it falls somewhere between 3.5 and 4.5 mph. Unlike interval training, tempo walking holds that elevated effort steadily rather than alternating hard and easy bursts.

Why it matters

Tempo walking builds cardiovascular efficiency — specifically your lactate threshold, the point at which effort starts to feel unsustainable. Training near that threshold raises it over time, meaning your "hard" eventually becomes easier. This translates to better endurance, a faster comfortable pace, and improved calorie burn compared to a standard leisurely walk.

It's particularly relevant for people who have plateaued with regular brisk walking and want a structured way to progress without running. It also fits well into a weekly routine alongside easier recovery walks and occasional intervals, giving your training variety and a clear purpose for each session.

Example

A 45-minute workout might look like: 5 minutes easy warm-up walk, 30 minutes at tempo pace (around 4 mph, breathing noticeably elevated but controlled), then 10 minutes of easy cool-down. The 30-minute sustained block is the tempo portion. Over several weeks, you might extend that block to 40 minutes or push the pace to 4.2 mph as fitness improves.

Common confusions

Tempo walking vs. brisk walking — Brisk walking is a general term for a pace faster than strolling, usually around 3–3.5 mph. Tempo walking is more specific: it targets a precise, sustained intensity zone rather than just "a bit faster."

Tempo walking vs. power walking — Power walking emphasizes exaggerated arm swing and specific technique to push speed up to 5 mph or more. Tempo walking is defined by intensity (heart rate zone or perceived effort) rather than form. You can do a tempo walk without the exaggerated mechanics of power walking.

Tempo walking vs. interval walking — Interval training alternates hard efforts with recovery periods. Tempo walking maintains a single, steady challenging pace throughout the main block. Both are useful, but they train slightly different physiological systems.
